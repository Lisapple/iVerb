Infinitive Form

The infinitive form is used in:
• Simple Present: Subject (S) + verb
Note: A s is added at the end of the verb for third person of singular (he, she and it).
• Simple Future: S + will + verb
• And all continuous forms finish with: verb+ing, as I'm eating.

Simple Past Form

The simple past (SP) form is used in used only for simple past: S + SP, as we came.
Note: For regular verbs, just add -ed at the end, as they talked.

Past Participle

The past participle (PP) is used in:
• Present Perfect Simple: S + have/has + PP
• Past Perfect Simple: S + had + PP
• Future Perfect Continuous: S + will + have/has + PP

Composition

The composition of verbs have been added to easily understand the structure of composed verbs, and should not be used for the global meaning. The last part of the composition is always an irregular verb, more common in general.
